Prescription Drugs

Prescription drugs are medications that are prescribed by a healthcare provider to treat a specific medical condition. These drugs must be taken according to the instructions provided by the healthcare provider to be safe and effective.

Over-the-Counter Drugs

Over-the-counter (OTC) drugs are medications that can be purchased without a prescription. These drugs are typically used to treat common ailments such as headaches, colds, and allergies. OTC drugs must be taken according to the instructions provided on the label to be safe and effective.

Illicit Drugs

Illicit drugs are substances that are illegal to possess, use, or distribute. Examples of illicit drugs include marijuana, cocaine, and heroin. These drugs can be highly addictive and can have serious negative effects on physical and mental health.

Alcohol

Alcohol is a psychoactive substance that is legal for adults to consume in most countries. However, excessive alcohol consumption can lead to negative health consequences and is a major cause of preventable deaths worldwide.

Tobacco

Tobacco is a plant product that is commonly used in cigarettes, which are a major cause of preventable deaths worldwide. Smoking tobacco can cause serious health problems, including cancer and heart disease.
